Deletions and Additions
Deletions

The scribe of ms Vatican marks deletions in several ways: a horizontal line striking through the word; a horizontal line above the word; dots above the word; diagonal strokes etc.

The Vatican scribe usually corrects errors on top of the word:

- Here the scribe wrote “אומר” - the next word in the text - and then corrected to “תחלה”.

- Here the scribe erased the word “את” that does not appear in the text with two diagonal lines above.

In order to signal that a word or letter in the manuscript has been deleted, mark the text using the mouse and then press on the (-) button under the text box.

Alternatively, press the delete button and then type the deleted text between parentheses.

- Here the deletion should be marked כרכי (הים) סדום

- Here the scribe wrote א and then corrected to ע. This needs to be marked: ה(א)עליונים

Unidentified deleted text

If the deleted word or letter cannot be identified, press once on the deletion button, to mark that something was deleted there and continue.

Additions

Additions can be either interlinear (above or below the line) or marginal (in the margins).

The scribe of ms Vatican usually inserts additions above the line if they are short, while longer passages are added in the margins.

- Here, the scribe has inserted the word לו that was inadvertently deleted during copying.

- Here the word צדקו was added in the margin and its accurate position was marked in above the line, after למען.

In order to signal an addition, press the [+] button below the text line and then write the added word between the brackets. If you want to signal that a transcribed word or letter should rather be handled as addition, please mark that word or letter with your mouse and then click on the [+] button. For example,

- The addition should be marked: אתה [רוצה] להודיע

Damaged or unreadable text

Manuscripts were damaged over the years. Sometimes they may be torn, and sometimes the ink was erased/blurred.

In order to mark unidentified letters (torn/blurred), press on the “damaged” button and insert spaces between square brackets according to the estimated number of missing/unclear letters. For example:

- אם יש ל <>

Uncertainty in transcription

‘doubtful’

The ‘doubtful’ button is intended for cases where you are unsure whether the automatic transcription correctly identified the letter in the manuscript. Please try to minimize the use of this button and use it only in extreme cases.

In order to mark the uncertainty, use the mouse to mark the uncertain letter and then press the “doubtful” button {?}. Alternatively, you can press the button and type the most probable option between curly brackets. For example:

- אם יש ל{עם}
