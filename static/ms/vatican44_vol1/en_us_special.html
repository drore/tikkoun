Special characters
Line fillers

In order to maintain a homogeneous text layout, medieval scribes frequently used “line fillers”, special characters added at the end of lines in cases where the text did not reach the end of line naturally. Another way was filling the remaining space with the first letter(s) of the word of the next line.

The scribe of the Vatican manuscript usually fills the end of lines with a mark that looks like “V” and sometimes with diagonal line.



In order to insert these characters at the end of a line, press the leftmost button at the buttons line under the text and select the V symbol or /

Wide letters

Another way to fill the rest of the line is to write very large letters at the end of the lines. These letters need to be written as regular letters and do not require any special character or marking. For example:

- שירים
Upper dots

Instead commas and full stops used today, the author of Vatican manuscript separated sentences using upper dots, one or two.:



To mark an upper dot press the button in the buttons line under the text. Add the same number of dots that the manuscript author wrote.

If you encounter a different mark that appears on the keyboard, for instance “:”, you may consider using it.

Ligature א-ל

Due to the sanctity of the divine name, scribes avoided to write two complete letters aleph lamed in continuation and wrote a ligature, a sign joining both letters, instead. We use for this the corresponding special sign ﭏ (note the little flag on the top left of the aleph).

Vatican scribe minimizes the use of this ligature and usually writes the letters regularly:

- Ligature א-ל

- and at the same line it appears also in regular writing.

Please use the special button below the text line and transcribe ﭏ. .

The Divine name

In manuscripts written by Jews, there are many ways to write the name of G”d replacing the tetragrammaton, most of them use two or three yods, sometimes with an ornament. The scribe of the Vatican manuscript, usually writes the divine name
Please use the special button יי below the text line in order to transcribe the Divine Name.

Abbreviations and Numbers

Some expressions are almost always abbreviated. For example:

ז'ש'ה' – זהו שאמר הכתוב; ד'א' – דבר אחר; ה'ב'ה' – הקודש (או הקדוש) ברוך הוא. שנ' - שנאמר

   

רישום נכון של ראשי התיבות:

Please write each single visible letter of the abbreviation without space in between, each letter followed directly by an apostrophe ‘ representing the dot above, i.e. : ה'ב'ה', ז'ש'ה'. Please do NOT resolve the abbreviations.

- א'ר' אבא

- ה'ב'ה'

Abbreviations should be written as is, using an apostrophe ‘ at the end of the word, do not complete the word

- שפה אחת וגו'

- כתי'

Vatican scribe uses a special character when the word is shortened at the letter מ:

 

This mark needs to be transcribed as a regular מ followed by and apostrophe: ‘מ

Characters that are used as a replacement to a numerical value should be transcribed as acronyms: 'ז', י'א

Headwords

The manuscript contains big headwords, usually written with square letters standing out from the other handwriting. Sometimes they invade the the line below that remains empty and sometimes they are integrated into the line.

Transcribe the headwords in regular font like the rest of the text
